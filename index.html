◆ DISCOVERY Listen · Build rapport · Understand who they are
OPENING
"What made you decide to jump on the call today?"
What got you interested in this?
What stood out that made you want to find out more?
What made you choose to speak with us specifically?
What are you hoping this could do for you?
Why now — what made this feel like the right time?
ABOUT THEM
Tell me a bit about yourself, Bob.
Tell me about life outside work.
Married, in a relationship, or flying solo?
Got any kids?
What do you do for work?
[Genuine reaction to their job] — that must be hard / interesting / demanding...
How long have you been doing that?
Do you enjoy it?
Lots of hours? Shift work? Weekends?
Do you commute? How long is that?
So when do you actually get time for yourself, Bob?
How long do you see yourself doing that for?

↳ "If this was truly important to you — could you find a way?"
◆ PAIN Find it · Stay there · Let them feel it
PAIN — SURFACE
What's the hardest part about that?
What's frustrating about that?
What impact is that having on your life?
What's the real cost of staying where you are?
What's that costing you?
TIME
How long have you been dealing with this?
How many years have you been living with this?
You've already been dealing with this for [X] years — could you do another [X]?
What would another 5 years of this look like?
How much longer are you willing to tolerate this?
TRIED
What have you tried so far?
Is this your first time doing anything like this online — or have you tried things before?
What happened?
Why do you think that didn't work?
In your opinion Bob — what do you think was missing?
What would have needed to be different for it to work?
What's stopping you from figuring this out on your own?
CONSEQUENCE
What happens if nothing changes?
How would you feel if you were still here next year?
How much longer can you realistically keep doing this?
At what point does staying where you are become harder than making a change?
What's the hardest part about knowing you're capable of more but still stuck?
If we're having this exact conversation a year from now — what does that look like?
If this was truly important — could you make it happen?
What would have to change for you to make this a priority?
At what point does this become important enough to act on?

◆ DREAM New vision · Make it real · Make it personal · Make them feel it
DREAM — OPEN
If this worked exactly how you hoped — what would change first?
What would life look like if this problem disappeared?
What would become possible?
What would a great day look like?
What would having that freedom mean to you?
MAKE IT PERSONAL
What would your family notice?
How would your confidence change?
Who else would benefit if you got this right?
What would getting this right mean for you personally?
What excites you most about that future?
MAKE IT REAL
If we were having this conversation 12 months from now and everything had gone perfectly — what would you be telling me?
How different would life feel if that became reality?
What would that mean for you and your family?
What would change first?

◆ ATM — QUALIFYING Handle now or handle as objections later
"Hey Bob — before we go any further, do you mind if I ask you a couple more questions about your situation?"
A — AUTHORITY
"Does she know you're on this call — or are we on a secret mission?"
When you make bigger decisions like this — do you take the lead or talk it through together?
If today felt right — is that a call you could make yourself?
T — TIME
"If we found exactly what you were looking for today — would you feel ready to move forward?"
Totally fair. What would you need to think about?
What's normally your process for a decision like this?
Is there anything specific you'd want clarity on first?
If this was important enough — could you make time for it?
If no time → "You've been dealing with this for [X] years. If you can't make it a priority now — when will you ever?"
M — MONEY
"Have you invested in yourself before?"
What was that experience like?
What did you like? What didn't you like?
What would you want differently this time?
"If you found exactly what you were looking for — is money actually the thing that would stop you? Or is it more making sure you're making the right decision?"
If 1 is money is a genuine concern and 10 is money wouldn't stop you — where would you put yourself?
Why that number and not lower?
What would need to happen for that number to be higher?
Is that a money thing or more of a certainty thing?
COMMITMENT
"Be honest with me — if this solved the problem we've been discussing, would you find a way or would you find a reason?"
What makes you say that?
How serious are you about solving this?
Why now?
Why has this become important?
◆ SUMMARY Repeat it back · Build certainty · Most closers skip this
"Bob — just so I make sure I've understood correctly..."
Summarise their pain in their exact words.
Summarise their frustrations.
Summarise their failed attempts.
Summarise their dream and goals.
Summarise the consequences of doing nothing.
"Have I got that right?"
"Based on everything you've told me — I genuinely think we can help. Would you like me to walk you through how?"
